Bookshelf & Inspiration

Reads & Recommendations

Deep Learning
Goodfellow, Bengio & Courville
The bible of the field. Dense, essential, and still the best grounding for diffusion models and representation learning.
Attention Is All You Need
Vaswani et al. (2017)
The paper that started everything. I re-read this before every new Transformer project.
The Art of Statistics
David Spiegelhalter
Brilliant at making statistical thinking feel natural, not mechanical. Changed how I approach EDA.
Denoising Diffusion Models
Ho et al. (2020)
DDPM. The paper behind my latent diffusion work. Elegant math, surprising results.

What I'd recommend to anyone entering AI/ML

Start with the math. Linear algebra and probability aren't optional prerequisites — they're the language. You can fake your way through a PyTorch tutorial but you can't fake your way through debugging a broken diffusion model.
→ Mahika's take on getting started
Build something from scratch. My NumPy neural net engine taught me more about backpropagation than any course. Implement Adam from scratch once — you'll never forget it.
→ On learning by doing
Don't skip data science on your way to deep learning. Understanding churn models, EDA, and forecasting makes you a better ML engineer — you know what problems actually look like in the wild.
→ On breadth vs depth
